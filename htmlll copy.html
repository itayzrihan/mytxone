Enhanced Liquid Glass Effect

Now with a crystal-clear center! The liquid distortion and chromatic aberration are gracefully concentrated towards the edges, giving the glass elements a more realistic lens-like or water droplet appearance. Scroll down to experience the effect.

Interactive Elements

Even interactive elements like buttons are morphed realistically as they pass behind the shapes, with the central clarity preserved.

In optics, a lens is a transmissive optical device that focuses or disperses a light beam by means of refraction. A simple lens consists of a single piece of transparent material, while a compound lens consists of several simple lenses, called elements, usually arranged along a common axis. Lenses are used in a wide array of imaging devices such as telescopes, binoculars, cameras, and microscopes.

Underlying Technology

This enhanced effect combines the power of SVG filters for distortion with CSS mask-image for precise control over the filter's visibility. A radial-gradient is used as the mask, creating a transparent center that gradually becomes opaque towards the edges. This allows the backdrop-filter to only apply its distortion where the mask is opaque, resulting in a beautifully clear middle and warped edges.

Cross-Browser Compatibility

The full distortion effect with a clear center is currently only visible in Chromium browsers like Google Chrome and Microsoft Edge. On Safari and Firefox, it gracefully degrades to a frosted glass effect with a clear center, maintaining a clean and modern look across all platforms.

Additionally, the phenomenon of chromatic aberration, where different colors of light are focused at different points, is a natural occurrence in lenses and contributes to the realistic edge distortion seen in this effect. This is subtly simulated by the `` using different scales for RGB channels.
